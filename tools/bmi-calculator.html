What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple ratio of weight to height: weight (kg) divided by height (m) squared. It's a quick screening tool for weight categories: under 18.5 is underweight, 18.5–24.9 is normal, 25–29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is in the obese range.

Limitations

BMI doesn't distinguish muscle from fat or account for age, sex, and body composition, so very muscular people may read as "overweight." Treat it as a rough guide, not a diagnosis, and consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice.
