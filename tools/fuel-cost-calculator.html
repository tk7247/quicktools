How fuel cost is calculated

In US units, fuel used equals distance divided by miles-per-gallon; multiply by the price per gallon for the total cost. In metric units, fuel used equals distance divided by 100, multiplied by litres-per-100km; multiply by price per litre. This tool handles both.

Plan trips and budgets

Use it to compare routes, budget for road trips, or estimate commuting costs. For round trips, enter the total distance both ways.
