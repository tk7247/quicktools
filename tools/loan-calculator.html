How loan payments are calculated

Monthly payments use the amortization formula: M = P · r · (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n − 1), where P is the principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12), and n is the number of monthly payments. Early payments go mostly toward interest; later payments go mostly toward principal.

Lower your total interest

A shorter term or a lower rate dramatically reduces total interest. Even small extra payments toward principal can shorten the loan and save money. Use this calculator to compare scenarios before you borrow.
