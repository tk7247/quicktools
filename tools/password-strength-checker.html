    What makes a password strong?

    Strength comes mainly from length and unpredictability. A password's "entropy" (measured in bits) estimates how many guesses an attacker would need: more characters and a larger mix of character types means higher entropy and a longer crack time. Anything below about 50 bits is weak; 80+ bits is strong.

    Tips for better passwords

    Use at least 12–16 characters, mix character types, avoid dictionary words and personal info, and never reuse passwords across sites. A password manager makes long, unique passwords practical.
