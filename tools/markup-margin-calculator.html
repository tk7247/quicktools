Markup vs. margin — what's the difference?

Markup is profit as a percentage of cost: (price − cost) ÷ cost. Margin is profit as a percentage of selling price: (price − cost) ÷ price. They describe the same profit from different angles, so a 50% markup is only a 33% margin. Confusing the two is a common pricing mistake.

Pricing your products

Use margin to understand how much of each sale you keep, and markup to set a price from your cost. Don't forget fees, shipping, and taxes when pricing for real profit.
