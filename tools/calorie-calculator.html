How daily calorie needs are estimated

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to estimate your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) — the calories your body burns at rest — then multiplies by an activity factor to get your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE), the calories needed to maintain your current weight.

Losing or gaining weight

A deficit of about 500 calories per day roughly corresponds to losing half a kilogram (about one pound) per week; a surplus does the reverse. These are estimates — individual results vary, and you should consult a healthcare professional before making major dietary changes.
