How to calculate sales tax

To add sales tax, multiply the price by the tax rate (as a decimal) and add it to the price. For example, 8.25% tax on $49.99 is $4.12, for a total of $54.11. To remove tax from a total, divide the total by (1 + rate) to recover the original pre-tax price.

Rates vary by location

Sales tax rates differ by country, state, county, and city, and some items are exempt. Enter your local combined rate for an accurate figure. This tool is for estimates — check your jurisdiction's official rate for exact amounts.
