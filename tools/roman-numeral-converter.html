How Roman numerals work

Roman numerals use seven letters: I (1), V (5), X (10), L (50), C (100), D (500), and M (1000). Values are added when larger or equal letters come first (VI = 6) and subtracted when a smaller letter precedes a larger one (IV = 4). The standard system represents numbers from 1 to 3999.

Common examples

4 = IV, 9 = IX, 40 = XL, 90 = XC, 400 = CD, 900 = CM. Years are a frequent use: 2024 = MMXXIV.
